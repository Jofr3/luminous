# Deck Building Rules

## Core Rules

1. **Exactly 60 cards** - A deck must contain exactly 60 cards. Not 59, not 61.
2. **4-copy limit** - You may only have up to 4 cards with the same name in your deck (except Basic Energy cards, which have no limit).
3. **At least one Basic Pokemon** - Your deck must contain at least one Basic Pokemon.

## Card Name Rules for Deck Building

- "ex," "EX," "GX," "V," "VMAX," "VSTAR," "BREAK," and "Mega" are part of a Pokemon's name. So Pikachu, Pikachu ex, Pikachu V, and Pikachu VMAX all have different names. You could have 4 of each.
- Regional prefixes ("Alolan," "Galarian," "Paldean," "Hisuian") are part of the name. Meowth and Alolan Meowth are different names.
- Owner names ("Brock's," "Rocket's") are part of the name.
- **Level is NOT part of the name.** Gengar, Gengar LV.43, Gengar LV.44, and Gengar LV.X all count as "Gengar."
- **Delta Species is NOT part of the name.** Aerodactyl and Aerodactyl δ are the same name.
- **Team Plasma is NOT part of the name.** 4 Team Plasma Liepard counts toward the 4-copy limit for all Liepard cards.

## Special Deck Restrictions

| Card Type | Restriction |
|-----------|-------------|
| Basic Energy | No limit |
| Regular cards | Max 4 copies per name |
| ACE SPEC Trainer | Max 1 total (not per name) |
| Radiant Pokemon | Max 1 total in deck |
| Prism Star cards | Max 1 copy per name |
| Pokemon V-UNION | Max 4 pieces total per name (all must be from same set/artist) |

## Guidelines for New Deck Builders

These are suggestions from the official rulebook, not strict rules:

- Pick one or two Energy types at most. Colorless Pokemon work in any deck.
- Include **12-15 Energy cards** in a 60-card deck.
- Include **20-25 Trainer cards**.
- Fill the rest with Pokemon.
- Try to have 4 copies of your key Pokemon and their Evolution chain.
- Every deck is different and will reflect your personal strategy over time.

## Format-Specific Rules

### Standard Format
- Only cards from recent expansions are legal.
- The rotation (which sets are legal) is updated annually.
- Check the official Play! Pokemon website for current rotation.

### Expanded Format
- A larger pool of cards is legal (generally from Black & White onward).
- Has its own ban list.

### Unlimited Format
- All cards ever printed are legal (subject to errata).

## Evolution Considerations

- If running a Stage 2 Pokemon, include its Stage 1 and Basic forms.
- **Rare Candy** can skip the Stage 1 to evolve a Basic directly to Stage 2 (but not on the first turn or on a Basic played that turn).
- Consider search cards (Nest Ball, Jacq, etc.) to find the Pokemon you need.
