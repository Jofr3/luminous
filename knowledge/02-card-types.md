# Card Types

There are 3 main categories of cards in the Pokemon TCG: Pokemon cards, Energy cards, and Trainer cards.

## Pokemon Cards

Pokemon are the core of the game. They battle in the Active Spot and can be placed on the Bench. Pokemon cards have the following attributes:

### Card Anatomy
- **Name** - The Pokemon's name (may include prefixes like "Alolan" or suffixes like "ex")
- **Stage** - Basic, Stage 1, Stage 2, or special stages (VMAX, VSTAR, BREAK, etc.)
- **Evolves From** - Which Pokemon this card evolves from (if any)
- **HP (Hit Points)** - How much damage the Pokemon can take before being Knocked Out
- **Type** - The Pokemon's elemental type (Grass, Fire, Water, etc.)
- **Attacks** - One or more attacks with an Energy cost, name, damage, and sometimes additional effects
- **Ability** - An optional special effect that is not an attack
- **Weakness** - A type that does increased damage to this Pokemon
- **Resistance** - A type that does reduced damage to this Pokemon
- **Retreat Cost** - The Energy cost to switch this Pokemon from Active to the Bench
- **Expansion Code / Collector Number** - Identifies which set the card belongs to

### Evolution Stages

- **Basic Pokemon** - Can be played directly from the hand onto the Bench or into the Active Spot. Basic Pokemon form the foundation of Evolution chains.
- **Stage 1 Pokemon** - Evolves from a Basic Pokemon. Place on top of the matching Basic Pokemon in play.
- **Stage 2 Pokemon** - Evolves from a Stage 1 Pokemon. Place on top of the matching Stage 1 Pokemon in play.

### Evolution Rules
- Neither player can evolve a Pokemon on their first turn.
- A Pokemon cannot evolve on the same turn it was put into play.
- A Pokemon cannot evolve twice on the same turn (evolving counts as "playing" it).
- When a Pokemon evolves, it keeps all attached cards (Energy, Tools, etc.) and damage counters.
- Any Special Conditions or attack effects on the Pokemon are removed when it evolves.
- An evolved Pokemon cannot use attacks or Abilities from its previous stage unless a card says otherwise.
- Both Active and Benched Pokemon can evolve.

## Trainer Cards

Trainer cards represent Items, Supporters, Stadiums, and Pokemon Tools. When you play a Trainer card, follow its instructions.

### Item Cards
- Can play as many as you want per turn.
- Follow the instructions on the card, then discard it (unless it says otherwise).

### Supporter Cards
- Can play only **one** Supporter card per turn.
- The player who goes first cannot play a Supporter on their first turn.
- Follow the instructions, then discard it.

### Stadium Cards
- Can play only **one** Stadium card per turn.
- Stays in play when played.
- Only one Stadium can be in play at a time. If a new Stadium is played, discard the old one.
- Cannot play a Stadium if one with the same name is already in play.

### Pokemon Tool Cards
- A subtype of Trainer card (as of Scarlet & Violet series, no longer considered Item cards).
- Attached to a Pokemon when played.
- Each Pokemon can have only **one** Pokemon Tool attached at a time.
- Remains attached unless the card text says otherwise or another effect removes it.

### Technical Machine Cards
- A kind of Trainer card (Item or Pokemon Tool) that attaches to a Pokemon.
- When attached, the Pokemon can use the Technical Machine's attack as its own.
- Remains attached unless the card text says otherwise.

### ACE SPEC Trainer Cards
- Extremely powerful Trainer cards.
- You may only have **one** ACE SPEC card in your entire deck (not one of each - one total).

## Energy Cards

Energy cards power Pokemon attacks. You normally attach one Energy card per turn from your hand to one of your Pokemon in play.

### Basic Energy Cards
There are 9 types of Basic Energy cards:
- Grass Energy
- Fire Energy
- Water Energy
- Lightning Energy
- Psychic Energy
- Fighting Energy
- Darkness Energy
- Metal Energy
- Fairy Energy

You can include any number of Basic Energy cards in your deck (they are exempt from the 4-copy rule).

### Special Energy Cards
- Provide special effects beyond just providing Energy.
- Subject to the standard 4-copy deck limit.
- Some provide multiple types of Energy or Energy with bonus effects.
- Cards that refer to "Basic Energy" do not include Special Energy unless specified.

### Colorless Energy Cost
Attack costs may include Colorless Energy symbols. Any type of Energy card satisfies a Colorless Energy cost. For example, an attack costing 1 Water + 2 Colorless can be paid with 1 Water Energy and 2 of any other Energy type.
