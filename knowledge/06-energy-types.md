# Energy Types

The Pokemon TCG has 11 Energy types. Each Energy type powers different attacks and corresponds to different Pokemon types. Pokémon in the TCG are grouped into these types, which consolidate the 18 types from the video games.

## The 11 Energy Types

### Grass
- **Video game types mapped:** Grass, Bug, Poison (some older sets)
- **Characteristics:** Grass-type Pokemon often have attacks that heal themselves or inflict Poisoned on opponents.

### Fire
- **Video game types mapped:** Fire
- **Characteristics:** Fire-type Pokemon have powerful attacks. They can inflict the Burned condition. Their attacks often require discarding Energy to use, needing time to build up again.

### Water
- **Video game types mapped:** Water, Ice
- **Characteristics:** Water-type Pokemon can manipulate Energy and move Pokemon around on the opposing team. Ice-type Pokemon from the video games are represented as Water-type in the TCG.

### Lightning
- **Video game types mapped:** Electric
- **Characteristics:** Lightning-type Pokemon can bring back used Energy from the discard pile. They can inflict the Paralyzed condition on opponents.

### Psychic
- **Video game types mapped:** Psychic, Ghost, Poison (in newer sets), Fairy (since Sword & Shield era)
- **Characteristics:** Psychic-type Pokemon are great for special Abilities. Their opponents often find themselves Asleep, Confused, or Poisoned.

### Fighting
- **Video game types mapped:** Fighting, Rock, Ground
- **Characteristics:** Fighting-type Pokemon can take bigger risks to do extra damage. Some can flip coins for combination hits.

### Darkness
- **Video game types mapped:** Dark, Poison (some sets)
- **Characteristics:** Darkness-type Pokemon feature sneaky attacks that often make opponents discard cards. They can also inflict Poisoned.

### Metal
- **Video game types mapped:** Steel
- **Characteristics:** Metal-type Pokemon can resist attacks for longer than most other Pokemon, often featuring damage reduction effects.

### Fairy
- **Video game types mapped:** Fairy
- **Characteristics:** Fairy-type Pokemon have tricks that make opposing Pokemon's attacks less effective.
- **Note:** As of the Sword & Shield era, the Fairy type was discontinued. Former Fairy-type Pokemon now appear as Psychic-type. Fairy Energy still exists in older card pools but is not printed in modern expansions.

### Dragon
- **Video game types mapped:** Dragon
- **Characteristics:** Dragon-type Pokemon have very strong attacks, but often require two different Energy types to use them. There are no Dragon-type Basic Energy cards; Dragon Pokemon use other Energy types.

### Colorless
- **Video game types mapped:** Normal, Flying, and various others
- **Characteristics:** Colorless-type Pokemon have diverse moves and work with any kind of deck. Colorless Energy symbols in attack costs can be satisfied by any type of Energy card.

## Basic Energy vs Special Energy

### Basic Energy Cards
- The 9 Basic Energy types are: Grass, Fire, Water, Lightning, Psychic, Fighting, Darkness, Metal, and Fairy.
- You can include **unlimited** Basic Energy cards in your deck (exempt from the 4-copy rule).
- Basic Energy cards provide one Energy of their printed type.

### Special Energy Cards
- Provide Energy with additional effects or provide multiple types of Energy.
- Subject to the **4-copy limit** per card name in a deck.
- Examples include Double Turbo Energy, Jet Energy, Luminous Energy, etc.
- Cards that specifically reference "Basic Energy" do not include Special Energy unless stated.

## Colorless Energy Cost

Many attacks include Colorless Energy symbols in their cost. These can be paid with **any type** of Energy. For example, an attack costing 1 Fire + 2 Colorless requires at least 1 Fire Energy but the remaining 2 can be any Energy type.
