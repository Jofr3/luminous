# Type Matchups: Weakness and Resistance

## How Weakness Works

If the Defending Pokemon has a Weakness to the Attacking Pokemon's type, the damage is increased. In the modern Scarlet & Violet era, Weakness **doubles** the damage (x2).

- Weakness is applied **after** attacker-side modifiers but **before** Resistance and defender-side modifiers.
- Weakness does NOT apply to Benched Pokemon.
- Weakness does NOT apply to damage counters placed directly by attack effects.

## How Resistance Works

If the Defending Pokemon has a Resistance to the Attacking Pokemon's type, the damage is reduced.

- In the Scarlet & Violet era, Resistance reduces damage by **30** (-30).
- In the Diamond & Pearl through Sword & Shield eras, Resistance typically reduced damage by **20** (-20).
- In the Original Series through EX era, and again in Scarlet & Violet, Resistance is **-30**.
- Resistance is applied **after** Weakness.
- Resistance does NOT apply to Benched Pokemon.
- Resistance cannot reduce damage below 0.

## Damage Calculation Order

1. Base damage
2. Attacker-side modifiers (before Weakness/Resistance)
3. **Weakness** (double the current damage if applicable)
4. **Resistance** (subtract 30 if applicable)
5. Defender-side modifiers (after Weakness/Resistance)
6. Final damage (minimum 0)

## Common Type Matchup Chart (Scarlet & Violet Era)

| Pokemon Type | Common Weakness | Common Resistance |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Grass** | Fire | - |
| **Fire** | Water | - |
| **Water** | Lightning | - |
| **Lightning** | Fighting | - |
| **Psychic** | Darkness | Fighting |
| **Fighting** | Psychic, Grass, or Water | - |
| **Darkness** | Fighting or Grass | - |
| **Metal** | Fire | Grass |
| **Dragon** | - (varies by card) | - |
| **Colorless** | Fighting | - |

**Note:** Individual cards may have different Weakness and Resistance than the common matchups listed above. Always check the card itself. Some Pokemon have no Weakness or no Resistance. Historically, type matchups have varied across different TCG eras and series.

## Dual-Type Pokemon

Some Pokemon have two types simultaneously. When a dual-type Pokemon attacks:
- If the Defending Pokemon has Weakness to **either** type, Weakness applies.
- If the Defending Pokemon has Resistance to **either** type, Resistance applies.
- If the Defending Pokemon has Weakness to one type and Resistance to the other, apply Weakness first, then Resistance.

## Historical Weakness Mechanics

- **Original Series through e-Card:** Weakness doubled damage (x2).
- **EX Series through HeartGold & SoulSilver:** Weakness doubled damage (x2).
- **Diamond & Pearl through Sword & Shield:** Weakness was printed on the card (usually x2, but sometimes +10, +20, +30, +40).
- **Scarlet & Violet onwards:** Weakness doubles damage (x2).
