# Special Conditions

Special Conditions are status effects that can only affect the Active Pokemon. There are 5 Special Conditions: **Asleep**, **Burned**, **Confused**, **Paralyzed**, and **Poisoned**.

## General Rules

- Special Conditions can **only** affect the Active Pokemon.
- When a Pokemon **moves to the Bench** (by retreating, switching, or any other effect), it recovers from **all** Special Conditions.
- When a Pokemon **evolves**, it recovers from **all** Special Conditions.
- Asleep, Confused, and Paralyzed are indicated by **rotating the card**. Since they all use rotation, only the most recent one of these three is in effect at a time.
- Burned and Poisoned use **markers** (placed on the card). These do not interfere with rotation-based conditions.
- A Pokemon can be Burned, Paralyzed (or Asleep or Confused), and Poisoned all at the same time.

## Asleep

- **Indicator:** Turn the Pokemon card counterclockwise (90 degrees).
- **Effect:** The Pokemon cannot attack or retreat.
- **Recovery:** During Pokemon Checkup, flip a coin. Heads = recover (turn card right-side up). Tails = stay Asleep.
- **Replaced by:** Confused or Paralyzed (since they also rotate the card).

## Burned

- **Indicator:** Place a Burn marker on the Pokemon.
- **Effect:** During Pokemon Checkup, place 2 damage counters (20 damage) on the Burned Pokemon, then flip a coin. Heads = recover (remove the Burn marker). Tails = stay Burned.
- **Stacking:** A Pokemon cannot have two Burn markers. A new Burned condition replaces the old one.
- **Note:** Burn markers must be visually distinct from damage counters.

## Confused

- **Indicator:** Turn the Pokemon card so the top faces toward you (180 degrees / upside down).
- **Effect:** When attacking, the player must flip a coin before the attack:
  - **Heads:** The attack works normally.
  - **Tails:** The attack does not happen, and the Confused Pokemon takes 3 damage counters (30 damage).
- **Note:** Confusion does NOT prevent retreating.
- **Replaced by:** Asleep or Paralyzed (since they also rotate the card).

## Paralyzed

- **Indicator:** Turn the Pokemon card clockwise (90 degrees).
- **Effect:** The Pokemon cannot attack or retreat.
- **Recovery:** The Pokemon recovers during Pokemon Checkup after its owner's next turn. (It stays Paralyzed for one full turn, then automatically recovers.)
- **Replaced by:** Asleep or Confused (since they also rotate the card).

## Poisoned

- **Indicator:** Place a Poison marker on the Pokemon.
- **Effect:** During Pokemon Checkup, place 1 damage counter (10 damage) on the Poisoned Pokemon.
- **Stacking:** A Pokemon cannot have two Poison markers. A new Poisoned condition replaces the old one.
- **Note:** Poison markers must be visually distinct from damage counters.
- **Note:** Some card effects cause "Badly Poisoned" or additional Poison damage (e.g., "place 4 damage counters instead of 1"). These enhanced Poison effects are still the Poisoned condition.

## Pokemon Checkup Order

During Pokemon Checkup, resolve Special Conditions in this order:

1. **Poisoned** - Place damage counters.
2. **Burned** - Place damage counters, then flip to recover.
3. **Asleep** - Flip to recover.
4. **Paralyzed** - Recover if the condition was applied before the Pokemon's owner's last turn.

After Special Conditions, apply any other between-turns effects (Abilities, Trainer cards, etc.). Then check if any Pokemon are Knocked Out.

## Removing Special Conditions

| Method | Removes |
|--------|---------|
| Move to Bench (retreat, switch, etc.) | All Special Conditions |
| Evolve | All Special Conditions |
| Card effect that says "remove Special Conditions" | As specified by the card |
| A new rotation condition (Asleep/Confused/Paralyzed) | Replaces the previous rotation condition only |
