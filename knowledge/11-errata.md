# Official Errata

This document lists official errata for the Pokemon TCG. Errata are corrections to card text published by The Pokemon Company International. When a card has errata, use the corrected text, not the printed text.

Last Update: January 22, 2026

## Major Changes to Existing Cards

### Pokemon Tool Cards
Starting with the Scarlet & Violet series, Pokemon Tool cards are **no longer considered Item cards**. All Pokemon Tool cards are in their own category of Trainer card: Pokemon Tool. This applies to ALL Pokemon Tool cards, including those from before Scarlet & Violet.

### Prism Energy
As long as this card is attached to a Pokemon, it provides Colorless Energy. If attached to a Basic Pokemon, it provides every type of Energy but only 1 Energy at a time.
**Change:** Now only provides Energy while attached to a Pokemon.

### Sacred Ash
Shuffle **up to 5** Pokemon from your discard pile into your deck.
**Change:** "Up to 5" means you can choose 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 cards.

### Leftovers
At the end of your turn, if the Pokemon this card is attached to is in the Active Spot, heal 20 damage from it.
**Change:** Heals 20 damage instead of 10.

### Super Rod
Shuffle **up to 3** in any combination of Pokemon and Basic Energy cards from your discard pile into your deck.
**Change:** "Up to 3" means you can choose 1, 2, or 3 cards.

### Superior Energy Retrieval
You can use this card only if you discard 2 other cards from your hand. Put **up to 4** Basic Energy cards from your discard pile into your hand. (You can't choose a card you discarded with the effect of this card.)
**Change:** "Up to 4" means you can choose 1, 2, 3, or 4 cards.

### Rare Candy
Choose 1 of your Basic Pokemon in play. If you have a Stage 2 card in your hand that evolves from that Pokemon, put that card onto the Basic Pokemon to evolve it, skipping the Stage 1. You can't use this card during your first turn or on a Basic Pokemon that was put into play this turn.
**Change:** Can no longer evolve Basic to Stage 1. Cannot be used on first turn. Cannot be used on a Pokemon played that turn.

### Potion
Heal 30 damage from 1 of your Pokemon.
**Change:** Heals 30 instead of 20.

### Great Ball
Look at the top 7 cards of your deck. You may reveal a Pokemon you find there and put it into your hand. Shuffle the other cards back into your deck.
**Change:** Can no longer search the entire deck for a Pokemon.

### Pokemon Catcher
Flip a coin. If heads, switch 1 of your opponent's Benched Pokemon with their Active Pokemon.
**Change:** Now requires a coin flip (functionally identical to Pokemon Reversal).

### Energy Retrieval
Put **up to 2** basic Energy cards from your discard pile into your hand.
**Change:** Gets back "up to 2" instead of exactly 2.

### Pal Pad
Shuffle **up to 2** Supporter cards from your discard pile into your deck.
**Change:** Shuffles in "up to 2" instead of exactly 2.

### Energy Recycler
Shuffle **up to 5** basic Energy cards from your discard pile into your deck.
**Change:** Shuffles in "up to 5" instead of exactly 5.

### Lum Berry
At the end of **each turn**, if the Pokemon this card is attached to is affected by any Special Conditions, it recovers from all of them, and discard this card.
**Change:** Works at end of each turn instead of between turns.

### Sitrus Berry
At the end of **each turn**, if the Pokemon this card is attached to has 3 or more damage counters on it, heal 30 damage from it and discard this card.
**Change:** Works at end of each turn instead of between turns.

### Hyper Potion
Heal 120 damage from 1 of your Pokemon that has at least 2 Energy attached. If you healed any damage in this way, discard 2 Energy from it.
**Change:** Completely new text.

### Quick Ball
You can play this card only if you discard another card from your hand. Search your deck for a Basic Pokemon, reveal it, and put it into your hand. Then, shuffle your deck.
**Change:** Completely new text.

### PlusPower
During this turn, your Pokemon's attacks do 10 more damage to the Active Pokemon (before applying Weakness and Resistance).
**Change:** No longer attaches to a Pokemon when used.

## Specific Card Printing Changes

For a complete list of specific card printing corrections across all series (Diamond & Pearl through Scarlet & Violet), see the full errata document at `resources/tcg_errata.pdf`. Notable examples include:

- **Cinderace** (Sword & Shield, 036/202): Retreat Cost corrected to 2 Colorless (was misprinted as 1).
- **Charizard** (Black & White—Plasma Storm, 136/135): Attack cost for Scorching Fire corrected.
- **Garchomp** (Sword & Shield—Brilliant Stars, 109/172): Protection only applies against opponent's attacks.
- **Cyrus Prism Star** (Sun & Moon—Ultra Prism, 120/156): Requires Active Water/Metal Pokemon (not just in play).
- **Minior** (Scarlet & Violet—Paradox Rift): Ability triggers each time an Energy is attached (not once per turn).
