# Advanced Rules

## Mulligans

If a player has no Basic Pokemon in their opening hand of 7 cards, they must take a mulligan:

### Both Players Have No Basic Pokemon
Both players reveal their hands, shuffle them back, and start over as normal. Neither draws extra cards.

### Only One Player Has No Basic Pokemon
1. That player announces a mulligan and waits for the other player to finish setting up.
2. The player with no Basic Pokemon reveals their hand, shuffles it into their deck, and draws 7 new cards. Repeat until they have at least one Basic Pokemon.
3. The player who did NOT mulligan may draw one extra card for each mulligan their opponent took beyond the shared count.
   - Example: If both took 2 mulligans, then Player A took 3 more, Player B may draw up to 3 cards. Any Basic Pokemon drawn may be placed on the Bench.
4. Both players reveal their Active and Benched Pokemon, and the game begins.

## Sudden Death

If both players achieve a win condition simultaneously, play a Sudden Death game:

- Set up a new game, but each player uses only **1 Prize card** instead of 6.
- Flip a coin to decide who goes first.
- The winner of this game is the overall winner.
- If Sudden Death also results in a tie, play another Sudden Death game until someone wins.

**Exception:** If one player wins in two ways (e.g., took last Prize AND opponent has no Pokemon) and the other wins in only one way, the player with two win conditions is the victor (no Sudden Death).

## "Up To" vs "Any Amount"

- **"Up to X"** (e.g., "move up to 3 damage counters") - You must choose at least 1 (between 1 and X). Exception: "draw up to X cards" allows choosing 0.
- **"Any amount" / "Any number"** (e.g., "discard any amount of Energy") - You may choose 0.
- **"You may"** - The action is optional; you can choose not to do it.

## Drawing More Cards Than Available

If a card tells you to draw or look at more cards from your deck than you have left, draw or look at however many remain. You do NOT lose. However, you lose if you cannot draw at the **beginning of your turn** - not if you can't draw due to a card effect mid-turn.

## What Counts as an Attack

Every attack has a cost and a name, and it might have base damage and effect text. Even if an attack does no damage (e.g., "Search your deck for a Basic Pokemon"), it is still an attack.

Abilities are NOT attacks. Using an Ability does not prevent you from attacking, and effects that reference "attacks" do not apply to Abilities.

## Pokemon Naming Rules

### Part of the Name
- Suffixes: ex, EX, GX, V, VMAX, VSTAR, BREAK
- Prefixes: Mega, Alolan, Galarian, Paldean, Hisuian
- Owner names: Brock's, Rocket's, etc.
- Symbols at the end of the name (e.g., star symbols)

### NOT Part of the Name
- Level (LV.X, LV.43, etc.)
- Delta Species (δ)
- Team Plasma designation

### Evolution Name Matching
The "Evolves from" text must match the Pokemon's name exactly. Graveler can evolve from Geodude or Geodude LV.12, but NOT from Brock's Geodude.

## Retreating Edge Cases

- You can retreat even if you have only one Benched Pokemon (that Pokemon moves to Active, and the Active moves to Bench).
- You cannot retreat if you have no Benched Pokemon.
- You can retreat and then still attack with the new Active Pokemon.
- Energy discarded for retreat goes to the discard pile.
- If a Pokemon retreats to the Bench, all Special Conditions and attack effects on it are removed.

## Knocked Out During Pokemon Checkup

If a Pokemon accumulates enough damage to be Knocked Out during Pokemon Checkup (from Poison, Burn, etc.), it is Knocked Out after all Pokemon Checkup steps are resolved. The opponent takes Prize card(s) and the player must promote a Benched Pokemon.

## Card Effects and Timing

- Effects that say "during your next turn" last until the end of the affected player's next turn.
- Effects on a Pokemon are removed when it moves to the Bench (unless specifically stated otherwise).
- If an effect says "the Defending Pokemon," it refers to the Pokemon currently in the Active Spot at the time the effect resolves.
- "When you play this Pokemon from your hand" effects trigger only on the initial play, not from search or other effects.

## Playing with Older Cards

Older card texts are interpreted according to current game rules and errata. If a card's text has been updated through errata, use the updated text. The key rules to follow are always the "Evolves from" or "Put this card into play" text on the card.
