# Pokemon Variants and Special Card Types

Throughout the history of the Pokemon TCG, many special categories of Pokemon cards have been introduced. Each has unique rules.

## Current Standard (Scarlet & Violet Era)

### Pokemon ex (lowercase)
- Powerful Pokemon with high HP and strong attacks.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.
- "ex" is part of the Pokemon's name (e.g., Miraidon and Miraidon ex are different names).
- Pokemon ex and Pokemon-EX (uppercase) are NOT the same.
- Can be Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2.

### Tera Pokemon ex
- A variant of Pokemon ex with a crystalline appearance.
- **Special effect:** Takes no damage from attacks while on the Bench (from both your and your opponent's attacks).
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.

### Mega Evolution Pokemon ex (2025 Mega Evolution Series)
- Reintroduced in the 2025 Mega Evolution expansion.
- Follow the **normal rules** of Evolution (unlike the original Mega Evolution mechanic which ended your turn).
- Can be Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2 depending on the Pokemon.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **3 Prize cards**.
- Do NOT require Spirit Link cards to evolve (unlike the original XY-era Mega Evolution Pokemon).

### ACE SPEC Trainer Cards
- Extremely powerful Trainer cards.
- Only **one** ACE SPEC card allowed per deck (not one of each).

### Ancient and Future
- Labels on Pokemon and other cards introduced in the Paradox Rift expansion.
- Ancient Pokemon focus on hard-hitting attacks.
- Future Pokemon focus on technical, precise attacks.
- Some Trainer and Energy cards interact specifically with Ancient or Future cards.

## Sword & Shield Era

### Pokemon V
- Powerful Basic Pokemon with high HP and strong attacks.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.
- "V" is part of the Pokemon's name.

### Pokemon VMAX
- Evolve from Pokemon V.
- Massive HP (often 300+) and powerful attacks.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **3 Prize cards**.
- Stage: VMAX (counts as an Evolution card).
- Still considered Pokemon V (effects targeting Pokemon V also affect VMAX).

### Pokemon VSTAR
- Evolve from Pokemon V.
- Each has a special **VSTAR Power** (an attack or Ability).
- A player can use only **one VSTAR Power per game** (tracked with a VSTAR marker).
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.
- Stage: VSTAR (counts as an Evolution card).
- Still considered Pokemon V.

### Pokemon V-UNION
- Comprised of **four separate cards** with the same name.
- Must play all four from the discard pile onto the Bench at the same time.
- Only one Bench spot, one Tool attachment.
- Each V-UNION can only be played **once per game**.
- NOT Basic Pokemon, NOT Evolution cards. Stage: V-UNION.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **3 Prize cards**.
- While in play, treated as one card. When not in play, each piece is separate.

### Battle Styles
- Pokemon labeled "Single Strike," "Rapid Strike," or "Fusion Strike."
- Interact with style-specific Trainer and Energy cards.

### Radiant Pokemon
- Special Shiny-colored Basic Pokemon.
- Only **one** Radiant Pokemon allowed per deck.
- "Radiant" is part of the name (Greninja and Radiant Greninja are different names).
- Considered a Pokemon with a Rule Box.

## Sun & Moon Era

### Pokemon-GX
- Powerful Pokemon with an exclusive **GX attack**.
- A player can use only **one GX attack per game** (tracked with a GX marker).
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.
- "GX" is part of the name.
- Can be Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2.

### TAG TEAM
- A special kind of Basic Pokemon-GX featuring multiple Pokemon.
- Very high HP and powerful GX attacks with bonus conditions.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **3 Prize cards**.

### TAG TEAM Supporter Cards
- Supporter cards featuring two Trainers.
- Offer an additional bonus effect if you discard extra cards when playing.

### Ultra Beasts
- Pokemon-GX labeled with an Ultra Beast marker.
- Some cards interact specifically with Ultra Beasts.
- Often have attacks related to Prize cards.

### Prism Star Cards
- Powerful cards (can be Pokemon, Trainer, or Special Energy) marked with a Prism Star symbol.
- Only **one** copy of each Prism Star card per deck.
- If a Prism Star card would go to the discard pile, it goes to the **Lost Zone** instead.

## XY Era

### Pokemon-EX (uppercase)
- Powerful Pokemon with high HP and strong attacks.
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards**.
- "EX" is part of the name. Pokemon-EX and Pokemon ex (lowercase) are different.
- Most are Basic Pokemon.

### Mega Evolution Pokemon (original)
- Evolved from Pokemon-EX. Stage: MEGA.
- When played, your turn ends (unless a Spirit Link Pokemon Tool is attached).
- When Knocked Out, your opponent takes **2 Prize cards** (same as their Pokemon-EX base).
- Primal Reversion Pokemon follow the same rules.

### Pokemon BREAK
- Special Evolution that keeps attacks, Abilities, Weakness, Resistance, and Retreat Cost of the previous stage.
- Gains new attacks/Abilities and may change HP or type.
- "BREAK" is part of the name.
- Stage: BREAK (counts as Evolution).

### Ancient Traits
- Special powers printed under a Pokemon's name.
- NOT attacks or Abilities (unaffected by cards that prevent attacks/Abilities).

### Team Flare Hyper Gear
- Pokemon Tool cards attached to the opponent's Pokemon-EX.
- Have negative effects on those Pokemon.

## Older Eras

### Pokemon LV.X
- Played on top of the same-name Pokemon, adding extra abilities.
- Level is NOT part of the name (Gengar LV.X has the same name as Gengar).

### Pokemon LEGEND
- Double cards showcasing Legendary Pokemon.
- Both cards must be played together at the same time.

### Pokemon SP
- Special Pokemon trained by a particular Trainer, with a symbol in the name.

### Restored Pokemon
- Fossil Pokemon with the "Restored" stage.
- Can only be put into play via a specific Fossil Item card.
- NOT Basic Pokemon, NOT Evolution cards.

### Dual-Type Pokemon
- Pokemon with two types simultaneously.
- Affected by anything that targets either type.
- Weakness and Resistance apply for both types.
