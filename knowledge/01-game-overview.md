# Game Overview

The Pokemon Trading Card Game (TCG) is a two-player strategy card game where each player builds a deck of 60 cards representing their Pokemon, Items, Supporters, and Energy. Players take turns battling their Pokemon against each other until one player achieves a win condition.

## Win Conditions

A player wins the game if any of these conditions are met:

1. **Take all Prize cards** - The first player to take all 6 of their Prize cards wins.
2. **Knock Out all opponent's Pokemon** - If your opponent has no Pokemon left in play (Active or Bench), you win.
3. **Opponent cannot draw** - If your opponent has no cards in their deck at the beginning of their turn and cannot draw a card, you win.

## Game Setup

1. Both players shake hands.
2. Flip a coin. The winner of the coin flip decides which player goes first.
3. Each player shuffles their 60-card deck and draws 7 cards.
4. Check for Basic Pokemon in hand (if none, take a mulligan - see [Advanced Rules](10-advanced-rules.md)).
5. Each player places one Basic Pokemon face down in the Active Spot.
6. Each player may place up to 5 more Basic Pokemon face down on the Bench.
7. Each player places the top 6 cards of their deck face down as Prize cards.
8. Both players flip their Active and Benched Pokemon face up and the game begins.

## Zones of Play

### Active Spot
The top row of a player's in-play section. Each player must always have exactly one Pokemon in their Active Spot. Only the Active Pokemon can attack. If your opponent has no Pokemon to place in the Active Spot, you win.

### Bench
The bottom row of a player's in-play section. Each player may have up to **5 Pokemon** on the Bench at any one time. Benched Pokemon can be moved to the Active Spot through retreating, switching effects, or when the Active Pokemon is Knocked Out.

### Deck
Each player starts with their own deck of exactly 60 cards. While both players know how many cards are in each deck, no one can look at or change the order of cards in either deck unless a card effect says so.

### Hand
Each player draws 7 cards at the start of the game. Your hand is hidden from your opponent. Cards drawn during the game go into your hand.

### Prize Cards
6 cards set aside face down from the top of each player's deck during setup. When you Knock Out an opponent's Pokemon, you take one (or more, depending on the Pokemon type) of your own Prize cards and add it to your hand.

### Discard Pile
Each player has their own discard pile. Cards taken out of play go to the discard pile unless a card says otherwise. When a Pokemon is Knocked Out, it and all attached cards go to its owner's discard pile. The discard pile is face up and anyone can look at it at any time.

### Lost Zone
A unique zone considered out of play. Cards sent to the Lost Zone cannot be recovered and are no longer playable during that game. The Lost Zone is distinct from the discard pile.

## First Turn Rules

- The player who goes first **cannot attack** on their first turn.
- The player who goes first **cannot play a Supporter card** on their first turn.
- **Neither player** can evolve a Pokemon on their first turn.
